# CompTIA-Security-
The 60-Day Security+ (SY0-701) Study Roadmap
Phase 1: Building the Foundation (Days 1–14)

Before you dive into security, you must understand the infrastructure you are protecting.

    Days 1–7: Networking Basics. Focus on how devices talk to each other. Learn the OSI model, IP addressing (IPv4/IPv6), subnetting, DNS, DHCP, and standard ports (HTTP, HTTPS, SSH, FTP, etc.).

    Days 8–14: Systems & Infrastructure. Understand the components of an enterprise environment. Learn the difference between servers and clients, what an Operating System does, virtualization, and cloud computing (IaaS vs. PaaS vs. SaaS).

    Resource Tip: Look for "Networking for Beginners" playlists on YouTube; they will save you hours of confusion later.

Phase 2: The Core Security Concepts (Days 15–30)

Now that you know how a network works, apply the "Security" lens.

    Days 15–22: Domains 1 (Security Concepts). Focus on the CIA Triad, the AAA framework, and the basics of cryptography (how encryption and hashing provide security).

    Days 23–30: Domain 2 (Threats, Vulnerabilities, & Mitigations). This is the "hacker" section. Study different malware types, social engineering, and the common ways software and hardware vulnerabilities are exploited.

    Active Learning: Start a glossary or a flashcard deck (Anki is great for this) for every acronym you encounter.

Phase 3: Defensive Architecture & Operations (Days 31–45)

This phase is the "meat" of the exam.

    Days 31–38: Domain 3 (Security Architecture). Learn how to secure a network. Study firewalls, intrusion detection systems, Zero Trust, and how to design a secure network topology.

    Days 39–45: Domain 4 (Security Operations). This is often the largest domain. Focus on Identity and Access Management (IAM), incident response procedures, and digital forensics. Learn the steps an organization takes when a breach happens.

Phase 4: Governance & Exam Readiness (Days 46–60)

Transition from a "technician" mindset to a "risk manager" mindset.

    Days 46–50: Domain 5 (Security Program Management). Study risk management, business continuity, disaster recovery, and data privacy regulations (like GDPR, since you are based in Belgium).

    Days 51–56: The Practice Test Marathon. Take full-length practice exams. Do not look at the answers immediately. After each test, grade yourself, and then spend hours reading why the wrong answers were wrong. This is the most important part of your preparation.

    Days 57–59: Targeted Review & PBQs. Review your weak areas identified by your practice tests. Watch "Performance-Based Question" (PBQ) walkthroughs on YouTube—these are the interactive scenarios that count for many points.

    Day 60: Light Review & Rest. Do not cram. Review your acronyms and ports list. Relax. You’ve done the work.

Three "Gold Rules" for Your Success:

    Consistency beats intensity. 90 minutes of focused study every day is significantly better than 10 hours of cramming on a Sunday.

    Teach it back. If you can’t explain a concept (like why HTTPS is more secure than HTTP) in plain English to a friend or an empty room, you don't know it well enough yet.

    Prioritize the "Why." If you understand how a system works, you don't have to memorize every single security rule—it will become intuitive.
